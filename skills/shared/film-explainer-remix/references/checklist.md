# Film Explainer Quality Checklist

Use this before finalizing a script, shot table, or rendered preview.

## Pass/Fail Gates

- Research order: serious film/series explainers have a research brief before scriptwriting.
- Benchmark boundary: reference explainers are used for structure and rhythm only, not copied wording.
- Source truth: every factual claim has a source timecode, visible clue, or transcript/OCR basis.
- Protagonist spine: the viewer can state who the piece follows and what that person wants.
- Hook: the first 8 seconds contain danger, contradiction, taboo choice, impossible result, or a direct mystery.
- First 30 seconds: the viewer knows who is trapped, what can go wrong, and why now.
- Turn density: every 15-25 seconds adds a new question, reveal, decision, or cost.
- Subtitle plan: each subtitle beat has emotion, connection type, visual need, and audio note before full render.
- Shot density: key narration lines use multiple shots; the edit is not just long movie chunks under narration.
- Visual match: shots are selected to support the current narration, not merely because they are next in source chronology.
- Audio hierarchy: narration is always intelligible; source audio and BGM never compete.
- Sync: narration, subtitle entry/exit, and rendered visual beat use measured audio duration or an explicit intentional pause.
- Subtitle readability: no dense paragraphs, no overlap with source hard subtitles, high contrast.
- External素材 boundary: non-source footage is never used as plot proof.
- Ending: the protagonist's final choice, cost, or changed understanding is clear.

## Script Kill List

Rewrite lines that sound like:
- `故事发生在...`
- `随后他开始了冒险`
- `一场更大的危机正在来临`
- `命运的齿轮开始转动`
- `他们展开了一场激烈战斗`
- `真相终于浮出水面`

Replace them with concrete action:
- what the protagonist sees
- what blocks the protagonist
- what rule is discovered
- what choice becomes unavoidable
- what price is paid

## Research And Remix Test

Fail the draft if:

- the plot was inferred only from sampled frames when the film/series title was available
- competitor explainers were merged into a new script
- the script repeats another creator's unique hook, title idea, analogy, or line order
- an interpretation is stated as confirmed fact
- external素材 is used as evidence for a source event

Pass only when:

- confirmed facts and interpretations are separated
- benchmark findings became constraints, not copied prose
- the new script has its own hook, wording, transitions, and emotional thesis
- every major reveal has a planned visual proof or source-audio bridge

## First 30 Seconds Test

Score each item 0/1:
- first frame is visually readable and emotionally charged
- first sentence creates a question
- protagonist appears or is clearly implied within 10 seconds
- danger or deadline appears within 15 seconds
- exposition is delayed until curiosity exists
- at least 5 distinct shots appear in 30 seconds
- one sound hit, silence beat, or visual emphasis marks the hook

If score is below 5, rebuild the opening.

## Sync Test

- Open the generated `*_sync_report.json`.
- Rewrite or split lines marked `narration_too_long`.
- Shorten holds, add a second sentence, or mark an intentional pause for lines marked `narration_too_short`.
- Re-render after timing fixes before judging whether the script or voice style is weak.

## Shot Test

For each narration beat, identify the shot role:
- proof: clue, screen, object, map, wound, device
- reaction: face, hesitation, fear, decision
- pressure: alarm, enemy, countdown, pursuit, locked door
- action: running, testing, hiding, repairing, attacking
- consequence: explosion, body cost, failed attempt, door opens, system changes
- bridge: external素材, atmosphere, map, abstract transition

If a beat has no role, cut or rewrite it.

## Final Watch Test

- Watch with sound only: the story should still be followable.
- Watch muted: shot order should still imply danger and progression.
- Watch at 1.5x mental speed: remove any administrative explanation.
- Watch the first 30 seconds cold: if there is no reason to continue, rewrite before polishing.
