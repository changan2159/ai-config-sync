# Packaging Recipes

Use when rendering or polishing a preview.

## Visual Rhythm

- First 30 seconds: prefer 0.6-2.0 second shots.
- Explanation sections: use proof shots and UI/object zooms to avoid static talking.
- Reversal sections: use one short silence, black card, freeze frame, or impact hit.
- Emotional closure: allow one longer hold if the face/action carries the meaning.

## Common Devices

- punch-in: small scale increase on protagonist reaction or clue
- freeze + label: pause on a clue for 0.4-0.8 seconds with a keyword
- black card: one-line sentence before a major reversal
- red box/arrow: highlight small UI/object evidence, use sparingly
- speed ramp: quick action bridge, not for plot-heavy moments
- glitch/flash: transition into alarms, screens, memory, surveillance, AI/system themes
- map/timeline card: compress travel or complex spatial logic
- split screen: compare clue vs later reveal

## Source-Audio Bridges

Use original sound at near-narration priority when it carries story force better than explanation:
- classic or famous line
- scream, breath, silence, alarm, impact, door, gunshot, explosion, crowd reaction
- emotional face hold where narration would weaken the moment
- plot handoff where the viewer needs to hear a short original response

Rules:
- Keep bridges short, usually 0.8-3.5 seconds.
- Do not let source dialogue and narration speak at the same time.
- Resume narration with a line that explains consequence, not a redundant paraphrase.
- If source dialogue is hard to hear, subtitle it separately or rewrite the beat as narration.

## Subtitle Packaging

- Use 1-2 lines.
- Keep line width short enough for mobile reading.
- Emphasize only high-value words: deadline, number, identity, rule, cost.
- Avoid covering faces, hands, key props, and source hard subtitles.
- If source has hard subtitles, crop, blur, mask, or move narration subtitles above them.

## Audio Stack

- Narration: primary.
- BGM: low enough not to mask consonants.
- Source audio: 10-20% under narration by default.
- Source-audio bridge: raise source audio to near narration priority while narration is silent.
- Effects: alarms, door hits, explosions, breath, screen beeps, impact hits.
- Silence: use sparingly before reversals or final choices.

## External素材 Rules

Allowed:
- abstract technology, city, weather, data, surveillance, crowd, fire, time, memory
- transition bridges between source shots
- mood reinforcement when source lacks a readable establishing shot
- short generated cards, glitch/flash, timer, map, waveform, surveillance UI, phone UI, warning UI, or chapter cards as packaging

Not allowed:
- showing a fake character action as if it happened in the film
- replacing a key source reveal with unrelated footage
- using copyrighted clips from another explainer as invisible filler
- adding facts not present in the source

When external footage is used, mark it in the shot table and keep the narration non-deceptive.
When generated effects or common transition clips are used, label them as `packaging` or `external素材`, not as plot evidence.
